LLOYDS BANKING GROUP PLC INTERNAL RISK PROFILE & COMPLIANCE FRAMEWORK – 2025
Prepared by: Group Risk Committee & Board Risk Committee
Date: 13 February 2026

OVERALL RISK APPETITE STATEMENT:
--------------------------------
"The Group does not tolerate non-adherence to regulatory and legal requirements. All colleagues employed by the Group are expected to comply with legal and regulatory obligations, requirements, statutes and permissions. Where inadvertent instances of non-compliance occur, these are promptly addressed with corrective action to minimise exposure and avoid recurrence."

ENTERPRISE RISK MANAGEMENT FRAMEWORK (ERMF) – PRINCIPAL RISKS:
-----------------------------------------------------------------
The ERMF defines the following Principal Risks:

1. CAPITAL RISK: The risk that an insufficient quantity or quality of capital is held to meet regulatory requirements or to support business strategy.
   - CET1 ratio target: c.13.0% (includes management buffer of c.1%)
   - Capital generation 2025: 147bps (178bps excluding motor finance charge)
   - Pro forma CET1 at year-end: 13.2%

2. CLIMATE RISK: The risk from the impacts of climate change and the transition to net zero.
   - Physical risks: acute events (floods, storms), chronic hazards (sea level rise)
   - Transition risks: policy changes, technology shifts, litigation, greenwashing
   - 2030 financed emissions targets (from 2020 baseline):
     * UK mortgages: -35% intensity (to 30kgCO2e/m²) – Progress: 42kgCO2e/m² (8%↓ from 2023)
     * Commercial real estate: -43% intensity (to 22kgCO2e/m²) – Progress: 34kgCO2e/m² (11%↓ from 2023)
     * Retail motor: -48% intensity (to 82gCO2e/km) – Progress: 132gCO2e/km (2%↓ from 2023)
     * Agriculture: -23% absolute (to 5.1MtCO2e) – Progress: 5.4MtCO2e (13%↓ from 2023)
     * Oil & gas: -50% absolute (to 3.6MtCO2e) – Progress: 1.6MtCO2e (59%↓ from 2023)

3. COMPLIANCE RISK: The risk of financial penalties, regulatory censure, criminal or civil enforcement action as a result of failure to comply with regulatory and/or legal requirements.
   - Focus areas: motor finance redress scheme, financial crime controls, Consumer Duty

4. CONDUCT RISK: The risk of the Group's activities having an adverse impact on outcomes for customers, undermining market integrity or distorting competition.
   - Motor finance provision: £1,950m (as at 31 Dec 2025, including £800m added in 2025)
   - FCA consultation on redress scheme ongoing (expected final rules March 2026)

5. CREDIT RISK: The risk that parties with whom the Group has contracted fail to meet their financial obligations.
   - Underlying impairment charge: £795m (up from £433m in 2024)
   - Total ECL allowance: £3,228m (down from £3,481m in 2024)
   - Stage 2 loans: 9.4% of total lending (down from 10.4%)
   - Stage 3 loans: 1.7% of total lending (down from 2.0%)

6. ECONOMIC CRIME RISK: The risk of ineffective policies, systems, processes and controls to prevent, detect and respond to fraud and/or financial crime.
   - External fraud accounts for 91% of operational risk events by volume
   - Two new Board-level risk appetite metrics introduced in 2025 (sanctions and fraud)

7. INSURANCE UNDERWRITING RISK: The risk of adverse developments in liabilities due to timing, frequency and severity of claims.
   - Life & pensions sales (PVNBP): £21,047m (up 15%)
   - General insurance combined ratio: 89% (down from 97%)
   - Weather-related claims: £111m (up from £82m)

8. LIQUIDITY RISK: The risk that the Group has insufficient financial resources to meet its commitments as they fall due.
   - LCR: 145% (well above 100% requirement)
   - NSFR: 124%
   - Loan:deposit ratio: 97%

9. MARKET RISK: The risk that the Group's capital or earnings profile are adversely affected by changes in market rates or prices.
   - Structural hedge: £244bn (up from £242bn)
   - Average daily trading VaR (95% 1-day): £2.2m

10. MODEL RISK: The potential for adverse consequences from model errors or the inappropriate use of modelled outputs.
    - AI governance frameworks being enhanced
    - Model validation required for all material models

11. OPERATIONAL RISK: The risk of actual or potential impact resulting from inadequate or failed internal processes, people and systems.
    - 91% of events by volume are external fraud
    - 81% of losses by value are "Clients, products and business practices" (conduct-related)
    - Total operational risk losses: within appetite

CAPITAL ADEQUACY ASSESSMENT:
----------------------------
The Group's total capital requirement (TCR) as at 31 December 2025 was £24,735m, comprising:
- Pillar 1 minimum: 8.0% of RWAs
- Pillar 2A: c.2.5% of RWAs (of which CET1 requirement: c.1.4%)
- Combined buffer requirement: CCB 2.5% + CCyB c.1.8% + O-SII buffer 2.0% (1.6% at Group)

Capital generation 2025: 147bps (178bps excluding motor finance charge)
The Board intends to pay down to CET1 target of c.13.0% by end of 2026.

STRESS TESTING:
---------------
The Group participated in the 2025 Bank of England Bank Capital Stress Test. The scenario tested:
- UK GDP fall of 5%
- Unemployment peak of 8.1%
- Inflation surge to 8%
- Interest rates peak at 8%
Result: The Group passed the stress test, performing strongly, with no capital actions required.
