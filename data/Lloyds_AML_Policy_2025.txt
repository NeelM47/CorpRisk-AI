LLOYDS BANKING GROUP AML & FINANCIAL CRIME POLICY – 2025
Applicable to all Banking and Commercial Divisions

1. CUSTOMER DUE DILIGENCE (CDD)
--------------------------------
- All corporate clients must provide proof of incorporation, beneficial ownership (>25%), and source of funds.
- Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) required for:
  a) Clients operating in high-risk jurisdictions (see Appendix A).
  b) Clients with complex ownership structures (shell companies, trusts).
  c) Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) and their associates.
  d) Clients in sectors with elevated money laundering risk.

2. TRANSACTION MONITORING
-------------------------
- Any single transaction exceeding $10,000 must be reviewed for AML flags.
- Multiple transactions within 30 days cumulatively exceeding $20,000 to/from high-risk jurisdictions trigger EDD.
- Wire transfers to/from countries in Appendix A must be screened against sanctions lists.
- Suspicious transactions are subject to investigation, customer contact, and potential rejection.

3. HIGH-RISK JURISDICTIONS (Appendix A)
----------------------------------------
- Bermuda
- Cayman Islands
- British Virgin Islands
- Panama
- UAE
- Any country on FATF grey list.

4. RED FLAGS
------------
- Unexplained large wire transfers from offshore jurisdictions
- Transactions involving shell companies or opaque offshore accounts
- Inconsistencies between stated business activity and transaction patterns
- Previous AML compliance issues or regulatory actions
- Beneficial ownership structures involving trusts in high-risk jurisdictions

5. ESCALATION
-------------
- If any red flag identified, file Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) within 24 hours.
- Material breaches of economic crime legislation have zero tolerance.

6. FRAUD PREVENTION
-------------------
- Fraud detection systems operate on all transactions (450m+ UK payments per month)
- Authorised Push Payment (APP) scams: reimbursement required for qualifying victims
- Geopolitical instability and evolving threat landscape require enhanced vigilance

7. SANCTIONS COMPLIANCE
------------------------
- Zero tolerance for breaches of sanctions regulations
- All payments screened against UN, EU, UK, and US sanctions lists
- In certain circumstances, risk appetite may be more conservative than legal obligations
