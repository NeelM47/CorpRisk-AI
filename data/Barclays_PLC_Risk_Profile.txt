ENTERPRISE RISK MANAGEMENT FRAMEWORK (ERMF) – PRINCIPAL RISKS:
-----------------------------------------------------------------
The ERMF defines 10 Principal Risks that the Group must manage:

1. CLIMATE RISK: The risk of financial losses arising from climate change through physical risks and risks associated with transitioning to a lower carbon economy.
   - Physical risks: acute events (floods, hurricanes, wildfires) and chronic hazards (sea level rise, heat stress)
   - Transition risks: policy changes, technology shifts, consumer sentiment, litigation

2. CREDIT RISK: The risk of loss from failure of clients, customers or counterparties to fully honour their obligations.
   - Key exposures: UK mortgages (£177bn), credit cards (£37bn), corporate loans (£133bn)
   - Elevated risk sectors: Energy, Transportation, Materials & Buildings, Agriculture

3. MARKET RISK: The risk of loss from adverse changes in value due to market variables.
   - Average daily Value at Risk (VaR): £18m (95% confidence, one-day holding)

4. TREASURY & CAPITAL RISK: The risk of insufficient capital or liquidity to support business activities.
   - Liquidity Coverage Ratio maintained at 170% (well above 100% requirement)
   - CET1 ratio of 14.3% (within target range of 13-14%)

5. OPERATIONAL RISK: The risk of loss from inadequate or failed processes, systems, human factors or external events.
   - 2025 operational risk losses: £143m (up from £127m in 2024)
   - 2,943 recorded events (increase from 2,392 in 2024)
   - External fraud accounts for 51% of losses; Execution, Delivery & Process Management accounts for 49%

6. MODEL RISK: The potential for adverse consequences from decisions based on incorrect or misused model outputs.
   - AI governance frameworks being enhanced
   - Model validation required every three years minimum

7. COMPLIANCE RISK: The risk of poor outcomes for customers, clients and markets from delivery of products and services.
   - Includes conduct risk, customer protection, data privacy, regulatory compliance

8. FINANCIAL CRIME RISK: The risk that the Group or its associated persons commit or facilitate financial crime.
   - Elevated to a Principal Risk effective 1 January 2025
   - Covers anti-bribery & corruption, anti-money laundering, tax evasion facilitation, sanctions

9. REPUTATION RISK: The risk that an action, transaction or event reduces trust in the Group's integrity or competence.
   - Key concerns: financing of sensitive sectors, human rights, association with controversial industries

10. LEGAL RISK: The risk of loss from failure to meet applicable laws, rules, regulations or contractual requirements.

MATERIAL EXISTING AND EMERGING RISKS:
---------------------------------------
i) Business conditions, general economy and geopolitical issues
   - US trade policies and tariff uncertainty
   - UK structural economic challenges
   - EU productivity and competitiveness concerns
   - China property market and deflation risks

ii) The impact of interest rate changes on profitability
    - Rate cuts pressure net interest margins
    - Higher-for-longer rates stress borrower affordability

iii) Competition in the banking and financial services industry
     - FinTech disruption
     - AI-driven competitors
     - Payments disintermediation

iv) Regulatory change agenda and impact on business model
    - Basel 3.1 implementation (delayed to 1 January 2027)
    - UK ring-fencing reforms
    - Consumer Duty requirements
    - Sustainability disclosure regulations (TCFD, CSRD)

v) Change delivery and execution risks
    - Strategic transformation programmes
    - Technology modernisation
    - Cost efficiency targets

vi) Evolving landscape with respect to artificial intelligence
    - Generative and agentic AI risks
    - AI governance and regulatory compliance (EU AI Act)
    - Cybersecurity threats from AI-enabled attacks

- G-SII buffer: 1.5%
- Pillar 2A: 2.7%
