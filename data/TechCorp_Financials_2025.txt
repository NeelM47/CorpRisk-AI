SIGNIFICANT TRANSACTIONS AND EVENTS – 2025
------------------------------------------------------------
1. On 15 January 2025, TechCorp received a wire transfer of
   $8,500,000 from "Shield Holdings Ltd", a company incorporated
   in the Cayman Islands with registered address at Ugland House,
   Grand Cayman. No public information is available on Shield
   Holdings' ultimate beneficial owners.

2. In March 2025, TechCorp repatriated $2,100,000 from its wholly‑owned
   subsidiary in Bermuda (TechCorp Bermuda Ltd.) as a dividend.
   The funds were used for general corporate purposes.

3. On 1 July 2025, TechCorp acquired 60% of the outstanding shares of
   "DataMind AI", a privately held artificial intelligence startup,
   for $15,000,000. The acquisition was financed through a combination
   of existing cash and a new $10,000,000 term loan from a syndicate
   of banks (see Note 5 – Debt).

4. During Q4 2025, TechCorp entered into a software licensing agreement
   with a Dubai‑based entity, "Gulf Tech Solutions FZCO", involving
   annual payments of $3,000,000. Enhanced due diligence was performed
   (see Risk Factors).

NOTE 5 – DEBT
------------------------------------------------------------
Long‑term debt at year‑end includes a $10,000,000 term loan drawn in
July 2025 to finance the DataMind AI acquisition. The loan bears
interest at SOFR + 3.5% and matures in 2030.

Additionally, TechCorp maintains a $15,000,000 revolving credit facility
with a UK‑based bank; $5,000,000 was drawn at year‑end (included in
short‑term debt).

NOTE 6 – RELATED PARTY TRANSACTIONS
------------------------------------------------------------
During 2025, TechCorp made payments totaling $1,200,000 to "Tamarack
Consulting", a firm owned by a trust whose beneficiaries include a
sibling of TechCorp's CEO. The transactions were approved by the
Audit Committee and are considered arm's‑length.

RISK FACTORS – MANAGEMENT'S DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS
------------------------------------------------------------
The Company operates in several jurisdictions that are considered
high‑risk for money laundering and tax evasion, including Bermuda
and the Cayman Islands. Management has implemented enhanced
compliance procedures, but these operations expose the Company to
reputational and regulatory risks.

The repatriated dividend from Bermuda and the receipt of a large
wire from an opaque Cayman Islands entity may trigger enhanced
scrutiny from financial partners and regulators. The Company has
disclosed these transactions to its banking partners and has
provided documentation of source of funds to the extent available.

In 2024, the Company paid a fine of $75,000 to the UK Financial
Conduct Authority for delayed reporting of suspicious transactions
related to its prior year operations. Management believes that
current controls are adequate to prevent recurrence.

Beneficial ownership of TechCorp is complex: approximately 40% of
voting shares are held through a discretionary trust domiciled in
the Cayman Islands, the "TechCorp 2005 Family Trust". The trustees
are a licensed trust company in George Town, Cayman Islands. The
ultimate beneficiaries are descendants of the founder, but the
trust structure does not publicly disclose individual names.

The Company relies on several offshore service providers for
accounting and legal support, located in Panama and the British
Virgin Islands. While these relationships are managed under service
agreements, they introduce additional compliance complexity.
